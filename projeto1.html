Johnson Solid

On this GitHub page I store the codes for my research on Johnson's solids, carried out for my undergraduate scientific studies. Explaining what these codes aim to do: After inserting the topological information of the solid you want, the Python code will create a function, where its global minimum will be the estimated solution, a solution obtained through the conjugate gradient method. All solutions are given in the form of coordinates, which, if placed in a three-dimensional space, will give the solid you reported previously. It is a project that aims to study Johnson solids, of which there are 92 specimens, of which not much is known about their nature;
